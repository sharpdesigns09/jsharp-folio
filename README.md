## About

I grew up in my family's AS9100 and ITAR-certified industrial tool and die machine shop in Canton, NC. Bridgeport mills, engineering blueprints, and the kind of tolerances you learn by making mistakes in steel.

Fifteen years later that thread runs through seed-stage startups, surgical robotics capstone projects, and a fabrication lab I helped redesign from the ground up. My path has never been linear: I taught myself embedded electronics, attended technical school for mechanical drafting, built my own computers and 3D printers from scrap, and founded a 3D printing startup before finishing my degree. What connects it all is a commitment to getting ideas from whiteboard to manufacturable reality — faster, and with the regulatory rigor clinical environments demand.

Today that work is converging around **Physical AI** — not using AI as a productivity tool, but embedding intelligence directly into manufacturing systems and medical devices. Predictive diagnostics. Computer vision quality control. Agentic fabrication workflows. AI-assisted design control documentation.

I am a designer first. The engineering follows the clinical need.
